Fitting Room

Add an conversational user interface (CUIs) for the retail space


PROJECT BACKGROUND

The project we work on was to research and prototype a novel conversational user interface (CUI) to improve the retail store dressing room experiences. A conversation user interace is a platform that mimic a conversation with a real human. Some examples include chatbots or voice assistant such as Google Home or Amazon Echo.

PROJECT CHALLENGE

When designing the CUI, we had to find the harmony between the interactive and functional form of the CUI. The interactive form allow the users to navigate through the different options and choices which is the functional form. The interactive form corresponds with the type of user input and system feedback. The functional form corresponds to a computational abstraction that defines the underlying interaction. In general, designers struggle to find a harmony between allowing users freedom to act and constraining user expressivity in order to increase the systems understanding and ability to provide what users want.

RESEARCH

To begin diving into the design, we did primary and secondary research. We started out by going to a couple retail stores to gauge a better sense of the current state. We observed how people were using the dressing rooms and how the service were in 4 different clothign stores. In addition, we actually went into try on clothes and documented our experiences. Our experience in all 4 stores were slightly different. In American Apparel, there were no employee on our floor and we were confused on the process since we were not sure if we should go in or not. On the other hand, when we were in Athleta, we had a very personalized experience where we exchanged names and had a conversation with one of the employee. When we asked for more sizes, the employee had a hand-held scanner to check the inventory.

KEY INSIGHTS
  • - Fitting rooms are important: They are the key step in converting causal shoppers into buyers and also help reduce return rates.
  • - Typical shopper will ask for advice from friends who accompany them or from other acquaintances through online interactions.
  • - 69% of women between 25-40 said changing rooms are stressful.
  • - Some concerns with the fitting room is that there are cramped conditions and inadequae lighting.
  • - Majority of shoppers agree that having self-help technologies improves their shopping experience.
DESIGN OPPORTUNITIES

The design opportunities for CUI include a faster service, a better shopping experience, and it can easily be integrated with other technology. Customers can quickly access information on store inventory and allow them to freely ask questions to the CUI instead of waiting for an associates to help. In addition, it can improve the fitting room experience to make it less stressful and more personalized to increase customer satisifaction. With the CUI, we can help customize the dressing room atmosphere such as lighting and noise to make it a more comfortable experience.


INITIAL SCENARIOS

Our initial idea was around changing the environment of the fitting room to make the customer feel more comfortable and showing them contexts in which their clothes could be worn. After some feedback, we realized that this was way too broad and also doesn’t particularly take advantage of the unique advantages that having a CUI would give the customer, so we went back and mapped the customer’s service journey through the lows and highs. We carryout several experience prototyping sessions to run through the scenarios in which potential users can interact with the CUI.

We decided to narrow our scope more from our feedback. We realized it would be difficult for the CUI to recommend outfits based on people’s different style choices, and we focused on jean fitting, as there is a lot to explore in the seemingly simple task of finding the right jeans including size and style.

CHANGE SCOPE TO JEAN FITTING

We noticed that trying on jeans can be a stressful experience.

  • - Experience of finding the right fit and type of jeans is traumatic for 43% of 1000 women surveyed.
  • - Customer is unsure whether a pair of jeans fits them well.
  • - Overwhelming amount of options in terms of size and style.
  • - Everyone’s body shape is UNIQUE



FINAL DESIGN

We created the Fashion Guru that serves as a personal dressing room assistant. It allows customers to ask questions and get advice when it comes to jean fitting. We incorporated movements into a test to help the user effectively check the fit of their jeans. With these movements, there is opportunity to use conversation to interact with the system rather than a GUI. We also gave customers the option of asking a sales associate to bring them new sizes or styles, thus keeping them in the fitting room, increasing convenience for them, and boosting sales.
