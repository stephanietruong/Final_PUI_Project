TrustyTech

A mobile app to optimize appointments with home service providers


ABOUT

PROJECT BACKGROUND

Businesses that provide home services such as plumbing, electrical and heating need to adapt to the changing expectations of customers. Many of the customers are dissatisfied with the service scheduling and delivery experience. Historically, scheduling a service visit to a home is done several days in advance. Customers are required to identify a day and a 4-hour “arrival window” for the service provider to visit. They’ll require customers to wait at the home during the entire time since they need them to explain their problem to the technician in greater details and authorize repairs. However, technicians often run late in between jobs and might not be able to make it to the customer’s home during the scheduled window of time.

Many customers aren’t willing to wait at the home during that huge chunk of time. Most of them are busy with work and they are not willing to or can’t take that much time off to stay at home. Therefore, many customers started giving service providers their mobile phone number so that they can be contacted when the technician is going to arrive. Yet, many technicians are forced to wait outside the customer’s home since many of them will be late coming back home. This causes further delays in the technicians’ schedules. Furthermore, some customers often wait for hours only to be told by the service provider that the technician will not be visiting their home that day. The challenge is to optimize the scheduling, rescheduling, joint decision making and service delivery to produce better experiences for both the customers, technicians, and service providers.

RESEARCH APPROACH

There were a lot of these gaps in our knowledge on this topic, we conducted multiple interviews and additionally researched existing services online. To learn more about how home service providers handle/schedule their requests, we interviewed local technicians. Additionally, we interviewed students who had recently hired a home service provider to gain insights into the customer's experience.


DEFINE

KEY INSIGHTS

After we completed and analyzed our research, we had some key insights that we used to motivated us to create our exploratory scenarios:

  • Customers have difficulty in distinguishing among service providers.
  • There are already many existing applications that aim to optimize the scheduling process, but customers are still constrained by having to wait at home for the technician during a large time window.
  • Most of the communication to customer is through the dispatcher and they need to get updates from the technician. Dispatcher need to understand where technicians are on the existing jobs to manage the expectations for customers. Most of the time, there are a lot of miscommunication.

EXPLORATORY SCENARIOS

We created 20 explanatory scenarios for our brainstorming sessions. These sceanarios helped us explore how a solution might deliver value to the two personas. Here was an example of one:

A) Problem/Context: Alyssa is fixing a toilet clog at a customer’s home but the problem turns out to be bigger than expected. She realizes she will not make it to her next appointment with Scott on time.
B) Solution: Alyssa uses an app to pass along her next appointment with Scott to an available colleague, and her colleague takes it over.
C) Resolution: Alyssa is able to finish her current customer’s request without missing/being late to her appointment with Scott.


IDEATE

STORYBOARDS

We narrowed down the scenarios and further developed what the experience would look like by developing storyboards and role playing the scenarios.

Value for Scott, the customer
Scott is able to take on an extra substitute job despite the unexpected heater issue. Our application frees him from the burden of having to wait around at home for a technician, which allows him to keep his schedule open and do the things that he actually wants.



Value for Alyssa, the home service provider/technician
Our application makes the employees’ jobs a lot more efficient because they no longer need to deal with customers rescheduling or cancelling appointments, or not being home at the scheduled time. They also no longer have to worry about running late to appointments.



Added value for Alyssa, the home service provider
The recorded videos may be used to train multiple new employees at once instead of having ridealongs which costs more time and money.



PROTOTYPE

SOLUTION

We decided to approach the problem from a different angle and focus on building trust between customers and home service providers so that we can eliminate scheduling and rescheduling problems altogether.

Our team created TrustyTech; a mobile application that aims to build trust between home service providers and their customers, in order to increase the comfort of unsupervised appointments. Through enabling home service appointments without the need of customer's presence, customers no longer have to worry about being at home during long appointment windows, and technicians are able to efficiently add more jobs throughout the day. This allows both parties to be more in control of their schedules.

Not only does TrustyTech resolve the issues of scheduling, it also provides an additional value to the home service provider since the recorded live stream videos could be used for training purposes. Thus, both the high costs of rescheduling and training costs could be reduced by the use of TrustyTech.


FEATURES
  • 1. Live Stream: Allow customer to view what the technician is doing
  • 2. Messaging / Chat: Technician can ask customer questions and allow them to authorize certain repairs virtually
  • 3. Technician Profile: Customer can be more comfortable with the technician if they learn more about them beforehand
  • 4. Progress Bar: Customer can track the different stages of the repair
