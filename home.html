Hebo

A personal post-operative companion and assistant

Patients recovering from skin cancer surgery often have numerous concerns about their post-operative treatment, which resulted in clinicians receiving a high volume of redundant and low-priority phone calls. Our team was tasked to find a solution that would better assist patients during this post-operative period. We conducted numerous stages of research, ideation, and prototyping to finally create Hebo, a post-operative care digital assistant that used voice, text, and images to convey care information to patients.

  • Role: Project Manager and UX Researcher
  • Duration: 5 months

TrustyTech

A mobile app to optimize appointments with home service providers

Many customers are dissatisfied with the service scheduling and delivery experiences with HVAC service providers. With TrustyTech, customers will no longer have to worry about being at home during long appointment windows, and technicians are able to efficiently add more jobs throughout the day. TrustyTech is a mobile application that aims to build trust between home service providers and their customers, in order to increase the comfort of unsupervised appointments. This allows both parties to be more in control of their schedules.

  • Role: UX Designer
  • Duration: 4 weeks

Employee Experience

Designing for employee retail experience for telecom client

On this project, I worked as a UX designer to understand more about the telecom retail experience for sales associates and store managers. The client wanted to deliver a better customer experience at their retail stores. The client built out an iPad app that they hope to soon replace their current desktop workstations. However, many sales associates were hesitant to use this new app. Therefore, I went into the retail stores to conduct interviews and observations on how sales associates interacted with their customers to identify pain points and opportunities for new features for the iPad app.

I also designed for payment kiosks at the retail store. Many customer comes into the retail store to pay their bill through the current kiosk. I redesigned the screens to make it more user- friendly and accessible for everyone.

Due to client confidentiality, please contact me for more information about this project.

  • Role: UX Designer
  • Duration: 2 months

Fitting Room Experience

A conversational user interface to enhance the retail experience


The goal of the project was to create a novel conversational user interface (CUI) to improve the retail store dressing room experiences. A conversation user interface was a platform that mimicked a conversation with a real human. Some examples of CUIs were chatbots or voice assistants such as Google Home or Amazon Echo. For our CUI, we designed a fitting room personal assistant that helped customers shop for jeans. The CUI would walk the user through steps to make sure their jeans fit and it also guided them through a series of small exercises to test out the jeans.

  • Role: UX Designer
  • Duration: 3 weeks

Caregiver's Experience

Reimagining the caregivers and their loved ones' experience

For this project, our team was given a particular target population to discover design opportunities. Our target population were 60 years old adults caring for their older loved ones. We conducted user research and gathered all our insights to identify 50+ problems and brainstorm 50+ solutions. At the end, we presented our findings and ideas to caregivers and their care receivers to get feedback.

  • Role: UX Designer
  • Duration: 3 weeks
